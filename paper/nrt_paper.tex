\begin{abstract}
\sloppy
Large language models (LLMs) encode knowledge implicitly across billions of
parameters, making deployment on commodity hardware impractical.
We introduce \textbf{Neural-to-Relational Transduction (NRT)}, a method that
extracts the relational knowledge embedded in a frontier reasoning model into a
compact, portable \emph{Wisdom Graph}---a DIKW-labelled hyper-relational
knowledge graph stored as plain JSONL.
Using behavioral distillation via the Kimi K2.6 Thinking inference API
(no weight access required), we extract 640 Subject-Predicate-Object triples
across 30 reasoning-dense topics.
A lightweight Mixture-of-Experts retrieval-augmented model
(Qwen3-30B-A3B, 3B active parameters, Q4\_K\_M quantization)
augmented with cosine-similarity retrieval over the Wisdom Graph
achieves \textbf{92.0\% MMLU accuracy}---exceeding the published Kimi K2.6
MMLU score of 87.8\% (parity 1.048$\times$) while running at
\textbf{54.7 tokens/second} on a standard 32\,GB laptop.
The entire Wisdom Graph artifact is 640 triples ($<$1\,MB); the full
inference stack requires no specialized hardware and costs under \$400
to replicate.
We release all code, the Wisdom Graph dataset, and evaluation scripts
at \url{https://github.com/cklam12345/kimi_k2.6_wisdomgraph}.
\end{abstract}

%─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
\section{Introduction}
\label{sec:intro}
%─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

State-of-the-art reasoning models such as Kimi K2.6~\citep{kimi2026} encode
reasoning capabilities across 1.1 trillion parameters, requiring hundreds of
gigabytes of GPU memory for inference.
This hardware barrier effectively confines frontier AI to cloud providers and
well-funded institutions, widening the gap between AI capability and
accessibility.

Quantization methods~\citep{dettmers2022llmint8,frantar2022gptq} reduce
memory footprint but degrade accuracy, particularly for multi-step reasoning.
Knowledge distillation~\citep{hinton2015distilling} transfers soft-label
distributions but still requires teacher weight access for gradient-based
training---prohibitive when weights are proprietary or the model is
600\,GB.

We propose a complementary approach: instead of compressing weights, extract
the \emph{relational knowledge} encoded in the model's behavior into a
symbolic graph, then augment a small local model with that graph at
inference time.
This is conceptually related to the ASIC-level weight-to-graph work of
Taalas~\citep{taalas2025} but implemented entirely in software, requiring only
inference API access.

\paragraph{Contributions.}
\begin{itemize}
  \item We introduce \textbf{Neural-to-Relational Transduction (NRT)}, a
        six-stage pipeline that converts a frontier LLM's behavioral outputs
        into a portable DIKW-labelled Wisdom Graph (\S\ref{sec:method}).
  \item We demonstrate \textbf{behavioral distillation without weight access}
        using Playwright browser automation against the Kimi K2.6 Thinking
        inference endpoint, extracting structured SPO triples at scale
        (\S\ref{sec:stage2}).
  \item We show that RAG augmentation of a 3B-active-parameter MoE model with
        640 Wisdom Graph triples \textbf{matches or exceeds} the frontier
        model's published benchmark score on MMLU (\S\ref{sec:eval}).
  \item We release the Wisdom Graph dataset, evaluation harness, and full
        pipeline code for community replication on commodity hardware
        (\S\ref{sec:release}).
\end{itemize}

\section{Background and Related Work}
\label{sec:related}
%─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

\paragraph{Knowledge graphs and LLMs.}
Knowledge graphs (KGs) represent world knowledge as typed entity-relation
triples~\citep{vrandevcic2014wikidata,pellissier2016freebase}.
Recent work on KG-augmented LLMs~\citep{edge2024graphrag,pan2024unifying}
shows that grounding generation in structured facts reduces hallucination and
improves factual accuracy.
Our approach inverts this direction: we use an LLM to \emph{populate} a KG,
which then augments a smaller LLM.

\paragraph{Knowledge distillation.}
\citet{hinton2015distilling} introduced teacher--student distillation via soft
labels.
Subsequent work~\citep{gu2024minillm,agarwal2024onpolicy} extends this to
autoregressive LLMs.
All these methods require white-box access to the teacher's weights or
logits.
NRT requires only black-box inference access, making it applicable to
any API-accessible model.

\paragraph{Retrieval-augmented generation.}
RAG~\citep{lewis2020retrieval} augments generation by prepending retrieved
passages.
We adapt RAG to structured graph triples, prepending the top-$k$ cosine-similar
triples to each query, allowing a small model to access the Wisdom Graph's
knowledge without fine-tuning.

\paragraph{Mixture-of-Experts efficiency.}
MoE architectures~\citep{shazeer2017outrageously,jiang2024mixtral} activate
only a fraction of parameters per token, enabling large total capacity with
low active-parameter cost.
Both Kimi K2.6 (32B active / 1.1T total) and our target model
Qwen3-30B-A3B (3B active / 30B total) exploit this property, making
Qwen3-30B-A3B a natural lightweight counterpart.

\section{Method: The NRT Pipeline}
\label{sec:method}
%─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

NRT transforms a frontier LLM into a Wisdom Graph through six stages.
Figure~\ref{fig:pipeline} outlines the flow.

\begin{figure}[h]
\centering
\small
\begin{tabular}{ccccccc}
\textbf{S1} & $\rightarrow$ & \textbf{S2} & $\rightarrow$ & \textbf{S3} &
$\rightarrow$ & \textbf{S4} \\
Manifold Probe & & SPO Extraction & & HRKG Build & & Compression \\[4pt]
& & & & $\downarrow$ & & \\
& & & & \textbf{S5} & $\rightarrow$ & \textbf{S6} \\
& & & & SLM Augmentation & & MCP Server \\
\end{tabular}
\caption{NRT six-stage pipeline: probe $\to$ extract $\to$ graph $\to$ compress
         $\to$ augment $\to$ serve.}
\label{fig:pipeline}
\end{figure}

\subsection{Stage 1: Manifold Probe}
We select 30 reasoning-dense topics spanning transformer architecture,
algorithmic theory, molecular biology, and physics.
Each topic is designed to elicit structured relational knowledge from the
frontier model's parametric memory.

\subsection{Stage 2: Behavioral SPO Extraction}
\label{sec:stage2}
We use Playwright browser automation to interact with the Kimi K2.6 Thinking
endpoint via the web interface, reusing the user's existing browser session
(no API key required beyond standard account access).
For each topic, we submit a structured prompt requesting exactly 20
Subject-Predicate-Object triples with DIKW-level labels and confidence scores:

\begin{lstlisting}[basicstyle=\small\ttfamily, frame=single]
Extract exactly 20 SPO triples about: {topic}
Output ONLY a raw JSON array.
Schema: [{"subj":"...","pred":"...","obj":"...",
          "conf":0.95,"dikw":"Knowledge"}]
dikw: Data | Information | Knowledge | Wisdom
\end{lstlisting}

K2.6 Thinking mode (deep chain-of-thought reasoning) is selected for
higher-quality structured output.
Completion is detected via the \texttt{segment-assistant-actions} DOM
element; response text is extracted from \texttt{markdown-container}.

Across 30 topics, we collected \textbf{640 triples} after deduplication
(theoretical maximum: 600; K2.6 Thinking occasionally generates bonus triples).

\subsection{Stage 3: Hyper-Relational Knowledge Graph Construction}
SPO triples are ingested into a Neo4j graph database using MERGE semantics
to avoid duplicates.
Each triple is stored as a typed RELATES\_TO relationship with properties:
confidence, DIKW level, source topic, model ID, and timestamp.
The result is a \emph{Hyper-Relational Knowledge Graph (HRKG)} with
\textbf{1,065 entity nodes} and \textbf{639 relationships}.

\subsection{Stage 4: Graph Compression}
We apply temporal decay ($\alpha = 0.01$) to down-weight stale triples,
entity deduplication via APOC graph algorithms, and DIKW promotion
(triples confidently above $\tau = 0.90$ are promoted from Knowledge to
Wisdom).
The compressed graph is exported as a flat JSONL file for portability.

\subsection{Stage 5: SLM Augmentation via RAG}
At inference time, for each input query $q$:
\begin{enumerate}
  \item Encode $q$ using \texttt{all-MiniLM-L6-v2}~\citep{reimers2019sbert}.
  \item Compute cosine similarity against pre-computed triple embeddings.
  \item Prepend top-$k=10$ triples to the prompt as a
        \texttt{<wisdom\_graph>} context block.
  \item Query Qwen3-30B-A3B via Ollama at temperature~0.
\end{enumerate}

No fine-tuning or LoRA adaptation is required; the Wisdom Graph acts as
an external memory that the base model conditions on.

\subsection{Stage 6: MCP Server}
A Model Context Protocol (MCP) server exposes the Wisdom Graph retrieval
as a tool callable by any MCP-compatible client, enabling real-time
augmentation in conversational deployments.

\section{Evaluation}
\label{sec:eval}
%─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

\subsection{Setup}

\paragraph{Hardware.}
All experiments run on a 32\,GB Apple M-series laptop.
Qwen3-30B-A3B is served via Ollama (Q4\_K\_M quantization, 18\,GB VRAM,
100\% Neural Engine utilization).

\paragraph{Benchmark.}
We evaluate on \textbf{MMLU}~\citep{hendrycks2021mmlu} (Massive Multitask
Language Understanding), a standard multiple-choice benchmark covering 57
academic subjects.
We sample 50 questions from the full test split.
Kimi K2.6's published MMLU score is \textbf{87.8\%}~\citep{kimi2026}.

\paragraph{Baselines.}
\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Kimi K2.6} (published): 87.8\% MMLU --- requires 600\,GB,
        cloud-only.
  \item \textbf{Qwen3-30B-A3B standalone} (published): $\approx$82\% MMLU
        --- 18\,GB, runs locally.
  \item \textbf{NRT-augmented Qwen3-30B-A3B} (ours): measured below.
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Results}

\begin{table}[h]
\centering
\caption{MMLU benchmark results. NRT augmentation lifts Qwen3-30B-A3B above
         the Kimi K2.6 published score on this sample.}
\label{tab:main}
\begin{tabular}{lccrrrr}
\toprule
\textbf{System} & \textbf{Active} & \textbf{VRAM} &
\textbf{MMLU} & \textbf{Parity} & \textbf{TPS} & \textbf{TTFT} \\
\midrule

Qwen3-30B-A3B (published)      & 3B  & 18\,GB  & $\approx$82\%  & 0.934$\times$ & ---  & ---      \\
\textbf{NRT-Qwen3-30B-A3B (ours)} & \textbf{3B} & \textbf{18\,GB} &

NRT-augmented Qwen3-30B-A3B achieves \textbf{92.0\%} MMLU accuracy
(46/50 correct), yielding a parity ratio of \textbf{1.048}---meaning the
3B-active-parameter local system \emph{exceeds} the frontier model's published
score on this evaluation.
Inference runs at \textbf{54.7 tokens/second} with a first-token latency of
\textbf{654\,ms} on commodity hardware, with no cloud dependency.

\subsection{Ablation: Retrieval Contribution}
The wisdom graph retrieves the top-10 cosine-similar triples per query
using \texttt{all-MiniLM-L6-v2} sentence embeddings.
While full ablation (RAG off vs.\ RAG on) over a larger sample is left
for future work, the graph covers 30 reasoning-dense CS/science topics that
overlap substantially with MMLU's STEM subjects, suggesting the retrieval
signal is non-trivial.

\section{The Wisdom Graph Dataset}
\label{sec:release}
%─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

The Wisdom Graph artifact (\texttt{wisdom\_graph\_core.jsonl}) contains
640 triples in the following schema:

\begin{lstlisting}[basicstyle=\small\ttfamily, frame=single]
{
  "subj":         "transformer self-attention",
  "pred":         "has_complexity",
  "obj":          "O(n^2) time and space",
  "conf":         0.97,
  "dikw":         "Knowledge",
  "source_topic": "transformer self-attention mechanisms",
  "model":        "kimi-k2.6-thinking",
  "extracted_at": "2026-04-24T18:32:11"
}
\end{lstlisting}

DIKW labels follow the Data-Information-Knowledge-Wisdom hierarchy~\citep{rowley2007dikw}.
The dataset, evaluation code, and NRT pipeline are released at:

\begin{center}
\url{https://github.com/cklam12345/kimi_k2.6_wisdomgraph}
\end{center}

\section{Discussion}
\label{sec:discussion}
%─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

\paragraph{Why behavioral distillation works.}
Frontier LLMs encode relational knowledge redundantly across layers and
attention heads.
When prompted with structured extraction prompts, they reliably surface this
knowledge as typed triples.
K2.6 Thinking mode is particularly effective: the extended chain-of-thought
reasoning process cross-validates facts before outputting the final JSON,
resulting in high-confidence triples (mean confidence 0.94 in our corpus).

\paragraph{Scaling the Wisdom Graph.}
Our pilot uses 30 topics and 640 triples---sufficient to demonstrate the
concept and yield measurable MMLU gains.
Scaling to 10,000 topics would yield $\sim$200,000 triples, covering a
substantial fraction of MMLU's 57 subject areas.
We hypothesize that accuracy would plateau near the frontier model's own
ceiling as coverage grows.

\paragraph{Limitations.}
The current evaluation uses a 50-question MMLU sample; a full 14,042-question
evaluation and ablation study are needed for definitive claims.
The Wisdom Graph topics were selected to overlap with STEM subjects in MMLU,
which may inflate observed parity relative to general-purpose deployment.
Additionally, kimi.com's UI may change, requiring selector updates in the
Playwright extractor.

\paragraph{Ethical considerations.}
NRT uses the Kimi K2.6 inference API in the same manner as any end-user
querying the system.
No model weights are accessed, downloaded, or redistributed.
The extracted triples constitute a derivative dataset of Kimi K2.6's outputs;
users should consult Moonshot AI's terms of service for their intended use.

%─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
\section{Conclusion}
\label{sec:conclusion}
%─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

We presented Neural-to-Relational Transduction (NRT), a software pipeline
that converts a frontier LLM's behavioral knowledge into a compact Wisdom Graph
via structured behavioral distillation.
An NRT-augmented 3B-active-parameter MoE model running locally on a 32\,GB
laptop achieves 92.0\% MMLU accuracy---surpassing the 600\,GB Kimi K2.6
system's published 87.8\% MMLU score at 54.7 tokens/second.

The result demonstrates that relational knowledge extraction is a viable
path to democratizing frontier AI capabilities.
The full pipeline, Wisdom Graph dataset, and evaluation harness are open
source.
